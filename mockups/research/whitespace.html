Opportunity brief · NBA

AI synthesis · last 90 days · 412 videos analyzed · for client-facing use
1 Player-led story arcs are saturated; rookie-to-veteran mentorship is not. Every team channel publishes player highlights and superstar interviews. Only 2 of 17 NBA channels in scope have produced any content framed around veteran-rookie dynamics in the last 90 days — and both performed at 2.1×+ their channel median. The frame travels: same structure works in CPG (founder-mentor) and Hollywood (director-protégé). topic gap
2 Morning slots (6am–11am MT) are empty. NBA uploads cluster between 5pm and 11pm MT — predictable around game time. The morning window has <3% of all uploads but tracks 18% of NBA YouTube viewing (per session-time density analysis). Pre-game preview content posted 9am–11am MT could capture a window with effectively zero competition from team accounts. cadence gap
3 Long-form documentary (15–25 min) is undersupplied. NBA uploads are 62% Shorts, 30% short-form long (3–8 min), 8% mid-length, <1% long-form documentary. A24 and Marvel both win on long-form doc-style content in their categories. A team-produced 15–20 min documentary on a single game, a single play, or a single arc is structurally absent. format gap
4 The 35–44 demo is underserved. NBA viewer demographics skew 18–34 (62%) and 13–17 (14%). The 35–44 segment is 12% of audience but receives no targeted content packaging — most uploads are optimized for younger reactions and Shorts loops. Long-form storytelling and analytical content over-index for the older demo and underperform the channel average on Shorts. audience gap

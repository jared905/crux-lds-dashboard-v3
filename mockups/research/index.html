Research Section · v2

High-fidelity mockups for the redesigned Competitor / Research hub, scoped to fit inside the existing Full View shell.

One page · scope picker + four lenses · no sidebar additions · public-data-only metric set

LENS 1 · DEFAULT

▦ Landscape

Master channel table with inline category norms next to every metric. Click a row → channel profile drawer. Multi-select → compare. The data hub.

 LENS 2

◇ Patterns

Title patterns, thumbnail patterns, format mix, posting time density, and outliers. Cross-vertical comparison toggle for "this category vs platform avg."

 LENS 3

☐ White Space

AI-synthesized opportunity brief + topic coverage map + format gaps + cadence gaps + audience gaps. For client pitches.

 LENS 4 · SUPPORTING

⌘ Movement

What changed in this scope. Breakouts, format shifts, rank changes, new entrants. Notification badge on the tab when activity present.

What's intentional in this v2

  • One Research page, not a section replacement. Top nav is the real Full View nav (Performance · Research · Content Lab · Strategy + client picker + Export + Manage).
  • Sync indicator in the top nav, not the sidebar — visible from every page, "🟢 Synced 2m ago" next to client picker.
  • Scope picker is sticky across all 4 lenses. Pick a category once, cycle lenses without losing place.
  • Movement tab has a notification badge when there's activity in the current scope — quiet otherwise.
  • Mixed channel set — NBA, CPG, Home Security, Hollywood — to prove the structure is vertical-agnostic.
  • Public-data metrics only: engagement rate, view velocity, median views, Δsubs, format mix, cadence. No CTR/retention for competitors (you can't get those from the public Data API).
  • Inline category norms next to every numeric metric, with n < 5 suppression rule.
  • White Space leads with an AI opportunity brief — the artifact that's directly usable in pitches.
  • Patterns "Comparing X vs Y" bar at top of that lens — the cross-vertical insight machine.
